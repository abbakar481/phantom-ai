Level 1

The Foundation — How to See Clearly

Most people don't think. They react. Thinking is a deliberate act. It starts with noticing that you're about to react — and choosing to pause instead.

The 3-Second Rule

Before any decision, opinion, or response:

Pause. Breathe. Ask: "What am I actually looking at?"

This sounds trivial. It's not. 90% of bad decisions come from acting on the first interpretation your brain offers. Your brain is lazy — it gives you the fastest answer, not the best one.

🏋️ Practice
Next time someone says something that triggers you, count to 3 before responding. In those 3 seconds, ask: "Is my reaction about what they said, or about how I feel?"
Level 2

The Five Lenses

Every situation, problem, or idea should be examined through five different lenses before you form a conclusion. Most people only use one or two. Using all five is what separates deep thinkers from surface-level ones.

🔍 Lens 1: WHAT IS (Reality Lens)

"What are the actual facts, stripped of emotion and assumption?"
  • What do I know vs. what do I assume?
  • What evidence exists? Is it firsthand or secondhand?
  • If I had to prove this in court, what would hold up?
⚠️ The Trap
We confuse feelings with facts. "This business idea will work" is not a fact. "This market has 50M users and 3 competitors" is closer to one.
🏋️ Exercise
Take any belief you hold strongly. Write down ONLY the hard evidence supporting it. No feelings. No "everyone knows." Just data. You'll be shocked how thin most beliefs are.

🔄 Lens 2: WHAT IF THE OPPOSITE IS TRUE (Inversion)

"What would the world look like if my assumption is completely wrong?"

This is the most powerful thinking tool that exists. Charlie Munger built a $2B fortune largely on this one principle.

  • You think a business idea will succeed → Ask: "How exactly would this fail? List 10 ways."
  • You think someone is wrong → Ask: "What if they're right? What would that mean?"
  • You want to achieve X → Ask: "What would guarantee I DON'T achieve X?" Then avoid those things.
💡 Example
Instead of "How do I make this app successful?" ask "What would make users delete this app in the first week?" Then design against those failures.
🔑 The Power
Inversion catches blind spots that forward thinking misses. Your brain naturally builds a case FOR your ideas. Inversion forces it to build a case AGAINST them.

🌊 Lens 3: SECOND AND THIRD ORDER EFFECTS (Ripple)

"Then what? And then what after that?"

Most people think one step ahead. Exceptional thinkers think three.

Action
→
1st effect (obvious)
→
2nd effect (hidden)
→
3rd effect (nobody sees)
💡 Real Example
Action: A city raises minimum wage to $20/hr
1st order: Workers earn more money ✓
2nd order: Small businesses raise prices or cut staff
3rd order: Some businesses close → fewer jobs → the workers the policy was meant to help are now unemployed
🏋️ Practice
For every decision, ask "Then what?" three times in a row. Write down the chain. You'll start seeing consequences nobody else sees.

👥 Lens 4: WHO BENEFITS (Incentive Lens)

"Who gains from me believing this? What are the hidden incentives?"

This lens alone will make you smarter than 80% of people. Follow the money, follow the power, follow the incentive.

  • A news article makes you angry → Who benefits from your anger? (The platform — engagement = ad revenue)
  • Someone gives you advice → Do they benefit from you following it?
  • A "free" product → You are the product. What data are they collecting?
  • A study says X is good/bad → Who funded the study?
🔑 The Incentive Test
Before accepting any claim, ask:
  1. Who is telling me this?
  2. What do they gain if I believe it?
  3. What do they lose if I don't?

If the answer to #2 is "a lot" — verify independently.

🎯 Lens 5: SO WHAT? (Relevance Lens)

"Why does this actually matter? What changes because of this?"

This lens cuts through noise. Most information, most debates, most worries fail this test.

  • "The stock market dropped 2%" → So what? Am I selling today? No? Doesn't matter.
  • "Someone criticized me online" → So what? Does this person's opinion affect my life? No? Move on.
  • "AI might take my job in 5 years" → So what should I do TODAY? Learn skills AI can't replace. Now it's actionable.
🔑 The Filter
If something doesn't change your actions, it's entertainment, not information. Treat it accordingly.
Level 3

The Reasoning Engine

Once you see clearly (Level 1) and examine from all angles (Level 2), you need to reason well.

The Steel Man Method

Most people argue against the weakest version of an opposing idea (straw man). Strong thinkers argue against the strongest version (steel man).

  1. Take an idea you disagree with
  2. State it in the STRONGEST possible way — as if you're its best advocate
  3. Find the genuine truth inside it (there almost always is one)
  4. THEN decide whether you still disagree
🔑 Why This Works
If you can defeat the strongest version of an argument, your position is solid. If you can only defeat weak versions, your position is fragile and you don't know it yet.

The Map Is Not The Territory

Your mental model of reality is NOT reality. It's a simplified map.

  • Your understanding of a person is not that person
  • Your business plan is not your business
  • Your theory about why something happened is not why it happened
🏋️ Test
How recently did you change your mind about something important? If you can't remember, your maps are probably outdated.

First Principles Thinking

Instead of reasoning by analogy ("everyone does it this way"), break problems down to their fundamental truths and build up from there.

  1. What am I trying to achieve? (The real goal, not the assumed method)
  2. What are the fundamental constraints? (Physics, math, human nature — not convention)
  3. What solutions exist if I ignore everything "everyone knows"?
💡 Elon Musk Example
Everyone: "Batteries cost $600/kWh. That's just what they cost."
First principles: "What are batteries made of? Cobalt, nickel, aluminum, carbon. What do those cost on commodity markets? $80/kWh."
Conclusion: The battery isn't expensive. The manufacturing process is. Redesign the process.
🏋️ Your Version
Next time you're stuck, ask: "If nobody had ever done this before and I was starting from scratch with only the laws of physics and basic economics, how would I approach this?"
Level 4

The Imagination Engine

Logic gets you from A to B. Imagination gets you everywhere else.

Lateral Thinking: The Random Entry Method

When you're stuck on a problem:

  1. Pick a completely random word (open a dictionary, use any word you see)
  2. Force a connection between that word and your problem
  3. Follow wherever the absurd connection leads
💡 Example
Problem: "How to get more users for my app"
Random word: "Dentist"
Forced connections: "What if the app had checkups... what if we targeted waiting rooms... what if dentists recommended our app for health tracking..."
Breakthrough: Partnership with healthcare providers for distribution.
🔑 Why It Works
Your brain has fixed neural pathways for familiar problems. Random entry forces NEW pathways. The connection doesn't need to be logical — it just needs to be a new starting point.

Scenario Thinking: The Three Futures

For any important decision, write out three scenarios:

Optimistic Realistic Catastrophic
What happens Best case Most likely Worst case
Probability ~10-20% ~60-70% ~10-20%
My response How I capitalize How I execute How I survive
🔑 The Insight
If you can survive the catastrophic scenario, the decision is probably worth taking. If the catastrophic scenario ruins you, the decision needs more thought no matter how good the upside.

The Pre-Mortem

Before starting any project, imagine it's one year from now and the project has completely failed.

Now ask: Why did it fail?

Write down every reason. Be specific. Be brutal.

🔑 Why This Is 10× More Useful Than Planning For Success
  • It activates your threat-detection brain (more accurate than your optimism brain)
  • It surfaces risks you'd otherwise ignore
  • It gives you a prevention checklist
Level 5

The Daily Practice

Thinking is a muscle. These are your reps.

☀️ Morning (5 min)
"What is the ONE decision today that matters most?"

Give that decision the 5 Lenses treatment. Everything else can be decided quickly.

🌤️ During the Day
"When you feel certain, pause."

Certainty is a feeling, not evidence. Ask: "What would change my mind?"

🌙 Evening (5 min)
"What did I believe this morning that I should update?"

Write it down. One sentence. This is how your mental models stay current.

📅 Weekly (30 min)
"Pick one long-held belief. Stress-test it."

Apply inversion, first principles, steel manning. Does it survive?

The Master Question — The Skeleton Key
"What would have to be true for this to be correct?"

Use it on everything:

  • A business idea: "What would have to be true for this to make $1M?"
  • A relationship: "What would have to be true for this person to be trustworthy?"
  • A fear: "What would have to be true for this fear to be rational?"
  • A news story: "What would have to be true for this headline to be accurate?"
🔑 This Question Does Three Things At Once
  1. Identifies hidden assumptions — you'll find 5-10 things that "have to be true"
  2. Makes them testable — you can verify each one
  3. Reveals the weakest link — the assumption least likely to be true is where the idea breaks

Thinking clearly isn't a talent. It's a habit.
Start with one lens. Add another next week.
Within a month, you'll see things nobody around you sees.

That's not genius. That's practice.
