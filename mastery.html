90-Day Mastery Plan

A systematic plan to permanently rewire how your brain reasons and imagines. Not theory. Daily practice with compounding results.

Foundation

What You're Actually Training

Your brain has two systems:

System 1 — Fast

Automatic, emotional, instinctive. Says "I like this" or "that's wrong" before you've thought about it. Runs 95% of your life.

System 2 — Slow

Deliberate, analytical, effortful. Does math, weighs evidence, considers possibilities. Powerful but lazy — only activates when forced.

Deep reasoning = making System 2 stronger and activating it more often.

Imagination = bridging System 1's pattern recognition with System 2's analytical power — so you can see things that don't exist yet.

🔑 Key Insight
Most people never train either one deliberately. The exercises below force both systems to work in ways they normally avoid. That discomfort IS the growth.
Phase 1 — Weeks 1-4

Destroy Your Default Thinking

You can't fill a cup that's already full. First, break the habits that keep you shallow.

Week 1-2: The Observation Protocol

Every day, pick ONE ordinary thing and analyze it for 10 minutes.

Not a big topic. Something small. A door handle. A traffic light. A price tag.

Ask these questions:

  • Why does it exist in this exact form? — A door handle is a lever because humans have hands that grip. If we had tentacles, doors would work differently. Every design is a frozen answer to a forgotten question.
  • What decisions led to this? — Someone chose this material, this color, this shape. Why? What were the alternatives? What constraints did they face?
  • What does this reveal about the system it's part of? — A $4.99 price tag reveals someone studied psychology and found $4.99 feels cheaper than $5.00. Pricing is theater. What else in your life is theater?
🏋️ Why This Works
You're training your brain to stop accepting the surface of things. After 14 days, you won't be able to look at anything without seeing deeper. It becomes automatic.

Week 3-4: The Disagreement Diary

Every day, find one thing you AGREE with — and argue against it.

Not something you disagree with. Something you agree with. Something "obvious."

Write 200-300 words making the strongest case AGAINST it.

Example 1

"Education is important" → Write the case that formal education is a waste of time

Example 2

"Hard work leads to success" → Write the case that luck matters more than effort

Example 3

"Follow your passion" → Write the case that this is terrible, dangerous advice

The Rule

Must be genuinely convincing. Not a strawman. A real argument that would make a smart person pause.

🔑 The Result
This is the single fastest way to develop intellectual flexibility. After 14 days, you'll see any issue from 3-4 angles instantly — because you've trained the muscle.
Phase 2 — Weeks 5-8

Build Your Reasoning Architecture

Defaults destroyed. Now install better systems.

The Daily Reasoning Workout (20 min/day)

Four 5-minute blocks. All four. Every day. Non-negotiable.

Block 1 · 5 min

⛓️ Causal Chain

Pick any event. Trace it backwards 5 levels (what caused it?) and forwards 5 levels (what will it cause?). Write the chain.

Block 2 · 5 min

🔬 Assumption Excavation

Take any statement you heard today. List every hidden assumption inside it. Most statements hide 5-15 assumptions.

Block 3 · 5 min

🔄 Perspective Swap

Take a situation. Write about it from: (1) someone who wants the opposite outcome, (2) someone 50 years from now, (3) someone in a completely different culture.

Block 4 · 5 min

❓ Impossible Question

Ask yourself an absurd question and seriously try to answer it. "How would you build a school with zero money?" Aim for unexpected, not correct.

💡 Assumption Excavation Example
Statement: "You should invest in real estate"

Hidden assumptions: Real estate will appreciate · You have capital or loan access · Local market is healthy · Property management is worth the time · Interest rates won't spike · You're stable enough to hold long-term · Alternatives (stocks, crypto, business) are worse · The advisor doesn't benefit from you buying

The Weekly Deep Dive (1-2 hours, weekends)

Pick ONE complex topic each week that you know nothing about.

Week 5

How does global shipping actually work?

Week 6

How do courts decide what's "fair"?

Week 7

How does a language evolve over centuries?

Week 8

How does a city's water system actually function?

For each topic:

  1. Read/watch for 30 minutes — get the basics
  2. Map the system — draw how the parts connect
  3. Find the non-obvious — what surprised you? What assumption was wrong?
  4. Connect to something unrelated — how does shipping relate to relationships? How does law relate to business strategy?
🔑 Why This Matters
You're building a library of mental models. The more systems you understand, the more patterns you recognize. A person who understands shipping, law, evolution, AND engineering sees connections in business that a pure business person never will.
Phase 3 — Weeks 9-12

Unleash Your Imagination

Reasoning without imagination is a calculator. Imagination without reasoning is a dream. Together, they're unstoppable.

The Synthesis Practice (daily)

Take two completely unrelated ideas and create something new from their intersection.

Idea A + Idea B = Something that doesn't exist yet
Therapy + Video Games

A game that adapts its narrative based on your emotional responses, providing therapeutic value through play

Farming + Blockchain

Consumers trace every ingredient to the exact field it grew in, with farmer reputation scores

Sleep + Education

Audio lessons calibrated to play during specific sleep stages when memory consolidation is strongest

The Rule

Spend 5+ minutes developing it. How would it work? Who'd use it? What problems does it create?

The "What If" Journal (10 min/day)

Write ONE deep "What If" scenario and explore it across 5 areas of life.

🌍 Example Deep What-If
"What if every lie made your skin glow for 24 hours?"

Politics: Campaigns become silent. No speeches, just actions and records.
Relationships: First dates become terrifying. "Do you like me?" has real stakes.
Business: Sales and marketing are revolutionized. Advertising can only be truthful.
Identity: People develop a culture of radical honesty or learn to speak in careful truths.
Crime: Courts become trivial. Interrogation is just asking questions in a dark room.

The Weekly Creation Requirement

Starting Week 9, create something every week that uses both reasoning and imagination.

  • Write a 500-word essay connecting two ideas nobody else has connected
  • Design a product that solves a problem in an unusual way
  • Write a scenario exploring a "what if"
  • Build a prototype (even a basic mockup)
  • Create a new framework or mental model
⚠️ The Rule
It must be new. Not a summary of someone else's thinking. Something from YOUR reasoning and YOUR imagination combining. This is where the magic happens.
After 90 Days

The Master Habits (Keep Forever)

Habit 1

📚 Read Outside Your World

20% of reading time on completely unrelated fields. Tech person? Read history. Business? Read biology. The breadth feeds the depth.

Habit 2

🗣️ Seek Different Thinkers

Not people who disagree politically. People who think differently structurally. A physicist and a poet solve problems in fundamentally different ways.

Habit 3

🧘 Protect Thinking Time

30 min daily. No phone, no music, no input. Just a problem and your mind. The discomfort is your brain being forced to generate instead of consume.

Habit 4

✍️ Write to Think

Don't just think in your head. Write it down. The gap between what you think you know and what you can articulate IS your growth edge.

Habit 5

🔄 Update Constantly

Keep a running list of things you've changed your mind about. Add monthly. If the list isn't growing, you're not thinking — you're defending.

The Truth Nobody Tells You
Thinking gets better by thinking. Deliberately. Uncomfortably. Daily. On problems with no clear answer.

Most people who want to "think better" consume more content. They learn about thinking. That's like watching workout videos to get strong.

Everything in this plan puts your brain at the edge of what you can currently figure out.

Stay at that edge. That's where everything changes.
